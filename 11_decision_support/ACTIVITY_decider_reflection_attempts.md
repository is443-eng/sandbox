# Activity Decider: Reflection Attempts

## Attempt 1
The AI did a good job turning messy venue descriptions into a clean table and giving a ranked shortlist quickly. It also responded to changed priorities by moving expensive and higher-capacity venues up, which shows it can adapt to new constraints. At the same time, it made a few shaky choices around exclusions and treated some missing details too confidently. Before trusting the final recommendation, I would manually verify price structure, true guest capacity, and whether each venue actually meets the catering and outdoor requirements.

## Attempt 2
I think the model's biggest strength was structure: it pulled key fields from long text and made comparison easier. It also showed useful reasoning when the couple's preferences changed between Stage 1 and Stage 2. The weak spot was consistency, because some venues were excluded or ranked in ways that did not fully match the rules. I would still use this as a first pass, but I would double-check missing data, hidden costs, and any assumptions about vibe or policy restrictions.

## Attempt 3
The AI handled extraction and summarization well, especially for capacity, pricing, and basic amenities. It also did what a decision tool should do by changing recommendations when the budget and guest count changed. Where it struggled was reliability: it sometimes inferred too much and was not fully consistent about exclusions. I would manually confirm uncertain fields and then use a weighted scoring sheet before making any real booking decision.
